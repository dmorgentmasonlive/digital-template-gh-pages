Capture the Point!

  • High Concept: Two players fight for control of an objective.
  • Player Experience Goals: I want players to have fun competing against one another, and also to enjoy the tug-of-war style gameplay with simple to learn casual controls.
  • Genre: The game is most similar to a fighting game.

The goal of the gameplay is to accumlate points by standing on the point. Players can force one another back to thier intial spawn point by jumping and pressing their down key to land on the other player. I initially planned to make the gameplay focus on aerial movement either through a flight mechanic or removing left and right ground movement. I decided not to prototype these mechanics as I am still learning the features of Phaser. Other core gameplay features not included in the prototype are the following: unique characters, playing to certain number of points, random events, and rounds of play.
  • Prototype goal: The goal of this prototype was to test the base capture-point gameplay. While the ability to fight the other players is included, it is not the focus of the prototype.
  • Features: The game includes gameplay similar to fighting games, but does not use hit points. Instead, gameplay revolves around gaining control of an objective, similar to a shooting game.
  • Setting/Premise: The game is currently an abstraction of two fighters trying to gain control of a power artifact.
  • Story: Two fighters are fighting for control of a powerful artifact.
  • Target Audience: The target demographic would probably be around ages 10+.
  • Play time: There is currently no win condition, but playing first to 1000 points takes 2-5 minutes.
  • Strategies: It can be more effective to move off the point for a moment to stop the enemy's approach than to try and hold your ground at all costs. Try luring an opponent into a game of chicken. Follow them as high as you can and see if they try to dive down too early. Use short hops by tapping up, left/right, down. This allows for movement while staying on the offensive.
  • Prototype Assets: Prototype sprites were made from scratch.
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: The gameplay is similar to mechanics in the game DiveKick. The game itself was derived from the included Phaser tutorial.
